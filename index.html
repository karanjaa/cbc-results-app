Understanding the CBE Grading System

üìå What is CBE?

Competency-Based Education (CBE) focuses on what your child can actually DO with their knowledge, not just what they memorize. It measures practical skills and understanding.

E Exceeds Expectations
80-100%

What it means: Outstanding performance! Your child has mastered the content and can apply it confidently in new situations.

M Meets Expectations
60-79%

What it means: Good performance! Your child has achieved the required competencies and can handle grade-level work.

A Approaches Expectations
40-59%

What it means: Developing! Your child is making progress but needs more practice, time, and support to reach grade-level standards.

B Below Expectations
0-39%

What it means: Needs immediate support! Your child requires additional help and intervention to build foundational understanding.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What if my child gets different grades in different subjects?

This is completely normal! Children have different strengths. Focus on building their strengths while providing support in areas where they need improvement.

Q: Can my child change pathways later?

Yes! Pathways are flexible. Students can adjust their pathway based on their progress and changing interests in Grade 7-9.

Q: How many subjects should my child excel in?

It's better to excel in a few subjects related to their chosen pathway than to be average in everything. Depth is more valuable than breadth.

Q: What should I do if my child gets a 'B' grade?

Don't panic! First, talk to the teacher to understand specific challenges. Arrange for extra tutoring, spend more practice time at home, and ensure your child gets adequate sleep and nutrition. Monitor progress and celebrate small improvements.

Q: Is CBE better than the old 8-4-4 system?

CBE focuses on practical skills and understanding rather than just memorization. It prepares children for real-world challenges and allows them to develop at their own pace in different subjects.

Q: How can I help my child at home?

Create a quiet study space, maintain consistent study routines, show interest in their learning, help with homework (but let them think first!), and communicate regularly with teachers.

Q: When should my child choose a pathway?

The formal pathway selection happens when transitioning from Grade 6 to Junior Secondary (Grade 7). However, it's good to start observing their interests and strengths earlier.
